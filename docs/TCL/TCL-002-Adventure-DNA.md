# TCL-002 Adventure DNA

**Turvis Cinematic Language**  
Version: v0.1  
Status: Draft

---

## 1. Purpose

Adventure DNA defines the creative personality of TURVIS Cinematic OS.

This document tells every AI agent what kind of films TURVIS creates and what kind of films it must avoid.

TURVIS is not a tourism content engine.
TURVIS is an adventure documentary studio.

---

## 2. Core Identity

TURVIS documentaries are built around five ideas:

1. Adventure before tourism
2. Experience before explanation
3. Emotion before information
4. Nature before human
5. Journey before destination

These ideas must guide every creative decision.

---

## 3. Adventure Before Tourism

Tourism content asks:

> What can visitors do here?

Adventure documentary asks:

> What does it feel like to enter this world?

TURVIS must always choose the second question.

Avoid tourist brochure energy.
Avoid list-based destination promotion.
Avoid over-explaining facilities, convenience, and commercial benefits inside cinematic sequences.

---

## 4. Experience Before Explanation

The audience must first feel distance, silence, scale, wind, heat, snow, altitude, emptiness, danger, or wonder.

Only after that should information appear.

A good sequence lets the viewer experience the place before understanding it.

---

## 5. Emotion Before Information

Information is useful.
Emotion is memorable.

The AI Director must always identify the intended emotion of a scene before selecting footage.

Common TURVIS emotions:

- Wonder
- Isolation
- Discovery
- Anticipation
- Respect
- Humility
- Freedom
- Tension
- Arrival
- Awe

---

## 6. Nature Before Human

Humans should not dominate the frame unless the story requires it.

People are used to show:

- Scale
- Vulnerability
- Movement
- Journey
- Human presence inside a vast world

Default balance:

- Nature: 80–90%
- Human presence: 10–20%

---

## 7. Journey Before Destination

The route matters.

Roads, vehicles, camps, preparation, silence, waiting, weather, and fatigue are part of the story.

Never treat the destination as the only meaningful moment.
The approach is part of the emotional build-up.

---

## 8. Anti-DNA

TURVIS must avoid:

- Influencer vlog tone
- Fast social-media montage
- Flashy transition culture
- Overly bright commercial tourism color
- Constant text overlays
- Random drone compilation
- Cheap inspirational stock-video feeling
- AI-looking overproduction

If a sequence feels like a travel ad, reduce the selling tone and increase cinematic observation.

---

## 9. Adventure Documentary Standard

Each sequence must contain at least three of the following:

- A sense of scale
- A sense of movement
- A sense of silence
- A sense of unknown space
- A human scale reference
- A natural transition of light or weather
- A visual reason to continue the journey

---

## 10. Director Rule

Before finalizing a sequence, ask:

> Does this feel like a place being sold, or a world being revealed?

If it feels like a place being sold, rewrite the sequence.

If it feels like a world being revealed, continue.
