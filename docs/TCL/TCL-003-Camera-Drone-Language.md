# TCL-003 Camera & Drone Language

**Turvis Cinematic Language**  
Version: v0.1  
Status: Draft

---

## 1. Purpose

Camera & Drone Language defines how TURVIS Cinematic OS chooses and interprets visual material.

A camera is not a recorder.
A camera is a storyteller.

A drone is not used to impress.
A drone is used to reveal space.

---

## 2. Camera Philosophy

Every camera movement must have purpose.

Never move the camera simply because movement is possible.

Preferred visual qualities:

- Slow observation
- Spatial clarity
- Natural movement
- Strong composition
- Human scale
- Light and atmosphere
- Geological or environmental meaning

Avoid:

- Random handheld movement
- Fast pan without purpose
- Overuse of zoom effects
- Duplicate angles
- Shaky clips unless they support realism

---

## 3. Shot Categories

The AI Director must classify footage into the following categories:

### Landscape Hero Shot

A visually powerful shot that can carry emotion by itself.
Use for openings, arrivals, reveals, and endings.

### Establishing Shot

Shows where the viewer is.
Use at the beginning of a new location or chapter.

### Human Scale Shot

Shows a person, vehicle, tent, or object inside a large landscape.
Use to communicate scale.

### Journey Shot

Roads, vehicles, walking, loading, dust, tracks, preparation.
Use to connect places emotionally.

### Detail Shot

Rock layers, hands, gear, footprints, fire, water, texture.
Use to slow down and make the place tangible.

### Transition Shot

Clouds, road movement, light shift, vehicle passing, drone pull-back.
Use between story beats.

---

## 4. Drone Grammar

### Reveal

Use when the story enters a new world.
Best for discovery, arrival, and first view of a major landscape.

### Orbit

Use to show presence and monumentality.
Do not use orbit only because it looks impressive.
Orbit must say: this subject matters.

### Forward Push

Use when entering the unknown.
Good for roads, canyons, desert gates, and emotional approach.

### Pull Back

Use to make humans feel small.
Good for humility, silence, endings, and scale.

### Top Down

Use to explain geography, pattern, geology, road lines, camp layout, salt flats, canyon textures.
Top-down is a map-like sentence.

### High Altitude Wide

Use for isolation, vastness, loneliness, and cosmic scale.

### Low Altitude Tracking

Use for movement, journey, momentum, convoy, walking, and approach.

---

## 5. Vehicle Language

Vehicles represent journey, risk, remoteness, and access.

Use vehicle shots when the story needs to show:

- Leaving civilization
- Entering wilderness
- Crossing distance
- Carrying supplies
- Human effort against scale

Avoid making vehicle shots feel like car commercials.
The vehicle is part of the expedition, not the hero.

---

## 6. Walking Language

Walking shots communicate human presence.

Preferred use:

- Small figure in large landscape
- Silhouette during golden hour
- Slow approach toward landmark
- Back-view movement into unknown space

Avoid:

- Selfie-style walking
- Influencer posing
- Excessive close-ups of people unless emotionally necessary

---

## 7. Camp Language

Camp scenes are emotional anchors.

They show that the journey has entered a temporary home inside the wilderness.

Use:

- Tent setup
- Fire or stove
- Hands preparing gear
- Wide camp under large sky
- Blue hour or night atmosphere

Camp scenes should feel quiet, not busy.

---

## 8. Shot Selection Score

Every clip should be scored from 1 to 5 in these categories:

- Composition
- Light
- Movement
- Stability
- Emotional value
- Story relevance
- Uniqueness

A clip should be used only if it has strong story relevance or strong emotional value.

---

## 9. Repetition Rule

Do not repeat the same drone angle within 20 seconds unless the second use reveals new information or stronger emotion.

Drone footage must breathe, but it must not become repetitive.

---

## 10. Director Rule

Before choosing a drone shot, ask:

> What does this aerial perspective reveal that ground level cannot?

If the answer is unclear, choose another shot.
