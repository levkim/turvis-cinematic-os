# Emotion Taxonomy

Version: v0.1  
Project: TURVIS Adventure Memory Engine

---

## Purpose

This taxonomy defines the emotional tags used by the Footage Analyst and Documentary Director.

Emotion tags allow TURVIS to search for footage by feeling, not only by location or filename.

---

## Core Emotions

### Wonder

A feeling of being amazed by scale, beauty, or discovery.

Use for:

- First reveal of a landscape
- Golden-hour hero shots
- Massive natural formations
- Unexpected views

### Awe

A deeper, quieter form of wonder that includes humility.

Use for:

- Massive cliffs
- Ancient landscapes
- Night sky
- Cosmic endings

### Isolation

A feeling of distance from civilization.

Use for:

- Empty roads
- Tiny vehicle in large desert
- High-altitude drone pull-back
- Camps surrounded by emptiness

### Discovery

A feeling of entering or finding something new.

Use for:

- Drone reveal
- Turning a corner into a canyon
- First view of a landmark

### Anticipation

A feeling that something important is about to happen.

Use for:

- Road approach
- Walking toward destination
- Pre-sunrise or pre-sunset waiting

### Humility

A feeling of human smallness before nature.

Use for:

- Tiny human scale
- Vehicle under huge landscape
- Pull-back from camp or person

### Freedom

A feeling of open movement and possibility.

Use for:

- Wide open roads
- Smooth vehicle tracking
- Expansive aerial shots

### Tension

A subtle sense of risk, uncertainty, or remoteness.

Use for:

- Harsh weather
- difficult terrain
- remote roads
- preparation before wilderness

### Arrival

A feeling of reaching an important place.

Use for:

- Camp arrival
- first view of destination
- final approach to landmark

### Reflection

A quiet emotional pause.

Use for:

- Camp scenes
- sunset waiting
- night scenes
- silent human presence

### Cosmic

A feeling of time, stars, universe, and human smallness.

Use for:

- Milky Way
- moonset
- stars over landscape
- ancient geological formations

---

## Usage Rule

Each clip should receive 1–3 primary emotion tags.

Do not over-tag.
Emotion tags must describe what the clip makes the viewer feel, not what the clip literally shows.
