# Footage Search Rules

Version: v0.1  
Project: TURVIS Adventure Memory Engine

---

## Purpose

These rules define how the Documentary Director searches Adventure Memory.

The Director should search by story purpose and emotion first, then technical qualities.

---

## Search Priority

When looking for footage, use this order:

1. Story beat
2. Emotion
3. Location
4. Shot type
5. Camera movement
6. Light quality
7. Overall score
8. Technical stability

---

## Search Pattern

A good search query should include:

```text
[story beat] + [emotion] + [location] + [shot type] + [light]
```

Example:

```text
arrival + awe + Bozzhyra + drone reveal + golden-hour
```

---

## Search Examples

### Example 1 — Isolation Beat

Narration:

> Beyond this point, there is nothing.

Search:

```text
threshold + isolation + empty road + drone pull-back + high altitude
```

Preferred result:

- tiny vehicle
- vast desert
- long road
- minimal human presence
- high emotional score

---

### Example 2 — Texture Beat

Narration:

> White chalk and red iron layers form the hills.

Search:

```text
texture + geological detail + Kyzylkup + top-down + layered hills
```

Preferred result:

- visible layers
- stable movement
- strong color separation
- high story relevance

---

### Example 3 — Arrival Beat

Narration:

> We finally reach the heart of the journey.

Search:

```text
arrival + awe + Bozzhyra + hero landscape + drone reveal
```

Preferred result:

- major landscape reveal
- strong composition
- emotional power
- cinematic pacing potential

---

### Example 4 — Cosmic Ending

Narration:

> I feel how small I am, and how deep the universe is.

Search:

```text
cosmic + humility + night sky + Milky Way + hero ending
```

Preferred result:

- stars or Milky Way
- landscape silhouette
- slow or static composition
- high ending value

---

## Reject Rules

Reject a clip if:

- It is beautiful but unrelated to the story beat
- It repeats an angle used recently
- It has poor stability and no documentary value
- It feels like influencer/vlog footage
- It distracts from the narration
- It weakens the emotional arc

---

## Hero Shot Rule

Hero shots should be reserved for:

- Opening
- Discovery
- Arrival
- Ending

Do not waste a hero shot on a weak story beat.

---

## Director Rule

> Search for the feeling first.  
> Search for the place second.  
> Search for the file last.
