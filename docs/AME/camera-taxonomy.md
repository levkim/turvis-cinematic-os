# Camera Taxonomy

Version: v0.1  
Project: TURVIS Adventure Memory Engine

---

## Purpose

Camera tags describe how a shot communicates visually.

They allow the Director to search by cinematic language:

```text
Find a drone pull-back that expresses isolation.
```

not only:

```text
Find clip001.mp4.
```

---

- mirrorless
- action-camera

## Shot Types

### Drone Reveal

A shot that reveals a hidden or upcoming landscape.

Best for:

- Discovery
- Arrival
- Opening

### Drone Orbit

A circular or semi-circular movement around a subject.

Best for:

- Monumentality
- Hero landscape
- Subject importance

### Drone Top Down

A vertical view from above.

Best for:

- Geography
- Pattern
- Geological texture
- Camp layout

### Drone Forward Push

A forward-moving aerial shot.

Best for:

- Entering unknown space
- Journey
- Anticipation

### Drone Pull Back

A shot moving away from the subject.

Best for:

- Scale
- Isolation
- Human smallness
- Ending

### Vehicle Tracking

A shot following or moving with a vehicle.

Best for:

- Journey
- Route
- Departure

### Walking Scale

A person walking inside a large landscape.

Best for:

- Human scale
- Approach
- Trekking
- Vulnerability

### Camp Detail

Close or medium shots of camp life.

Best for:

- Rest
- Reflection
- Human presence
- Temporary home

### Geological Detail

A shot focused on rocks, layers, snow, sand, water, or terrain texture.

Best for:

- Texture beat
- Explanation through image
- Natural history

### Hero Landscape

A powerful wide shot that can carry emotion by itself.

Best for:

- Opening
- Arrival
- Ending
- Main landmark

### Night Sky

Stars, Milky Way, moon, blue hour, or night camp.

Best for:

- Cosmic beat
- Reflection
- Ending

## Usage Rule

Camera tags must describe visual function, not only equipment.

A drone shot is not automatically valuable.
It is valuable only when it reveals space, scale, movement, or emotion.
